Solving Linear Inequalities

From Basics to Advanced Problems

What are Linear Inequalities?

A linear inequality is a mathematical statement that relates a linear expression as either less than, greater than, less than or equal to, or greater than or equal to another linear expression.

Examples of linear inequalities:

  • x > 5 (x is greater than 5)
  • 2y + 3 ≤ 11 (2y plus 3 is less than or equal to 11)
  • -3z + 2 ≥ 4z - 5 (-3z plus 2 is greater than or equal to 4z minus 5)

Inequality Symbols

Symbol Meaning Example
< Less than x < 3
> Greater than x > 3
≤ Less than or equal to x ≤ 3
≥ Greater than or equal to x ≥ 3

Important Rule!

When multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number, the inequality sign must be reversed.

Example: If -2x > 6, then x < -3 (sign reversed from > to <)

One-Step Inequalities

One-step inequalities can be solved with a single operation. Remember the special rule for negative coefficients!

Example 1: x + 3 > 7

x + 3 > 7
x + 3 - 3 > 7 - 3 (Subtract 3 from both sides)
x > 4

Solution: All numbers greater than 4.

Example 2: -2x ≤ 8

-2x ≤ 8
-2x ÷ (-2) ≥ 8 ÷ (-2) (Divide both sides by -2, reverse the sign)
x ≥ -4

Solution: All numbers greater than or equal to -4.

Remember!

When multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must reverse the inequality sign.

Practice Problem

Solve: -5x > 15

-5x > 15
-5x ÷ (-5) < 15 ÷ (-5) (Divide by -5, reverse the sign)
x < -3

Solution: All numbers less than -3.

Multi-Step Inequalities

Multi-step inequalities require multiple operations to solve. Follow the same steps as solving equations, but remember to reverse the inequality sign if you multiply or divide by a negative number.

Example 1: 3x + 5 < 14

3x + 5 < 14
3x + 5 - 5 < 14 - 5 (Subtract 5 from both sides)
3x < 9
3x ÷ 3 < 9 ÷ 3 (Divide both sides by 3)
x < 3

Solution: All numbers less than 3.

Example 2: 4 - 2x ≥ 10

4 - 2x ≥ 10
4 - 2x - 4 ≥ 10 - 4 (Subtract 4 from both sides)
-2x ≥ 6
-2x ÷ (-2) ≤ 6 ÷ (-2) (Divide by -2, reverse the sign)
x ≤ -3

Solution: All numbers less than or equal to -3.

Practice Problem

Solve: 2(x - 3) + 5 ≥ 7

2(x - 3) + 5 ≥ 7
2x - 6 + 5 ≥ 7 (Distribute)
2x - 1 ≥ 7 (Combine like terms)
2x - 1 + 1 ≥ 7 + 1 (Add 1 to both sides)
2x ≥ 8
2x ÷ 2 ≥ 8 ÷ 2 (Divide both sides by 2)
x ≥ 4

Solution: All numbers greater than or equal to 4.

Compound Inequalities

Compound inequalities are two inequalities joined by "and" or "or".

  • "And" means both conditions must be true (intersection)
  • "Or" means either condition can be true (union)

Example 1: And Inequality (-2 ≤ x < 3)

This means x ≥ -2 AND x < 3
Solve each part separately:
x ≥ -2
x < 3

Solution: All numbers between -2 and 3, including -2 but not including 3.

Example 2: Or Inequality (x ≤ -1 or x > 2)

This means x ≤ -1 OR x > 2
Solve each part separately:
x ≤ -1
x > 2

Solution: All numbers less than or equal to -1 or greater than 2.

Practice Problem

Solve: -3 ≤ 2x + 1 < 5

-3 ≤ 2x + 1 < 5
-3 - 1 ≤ 2x + 1 - 1 < 5 - 1 (Subtract 1 from all parts)
-4 ≤ 2x < 4
-4 ÷ 2 ≤ 2x ÷ 2 < 4 ÷ 2 (Divide all parts by 2)
-2 ≤ x < 2

Solution: All numbers between -2 and 2, including -2 but not including 2.

Practice Problems

Test your understanding with these practice problems. Try to solve them on your own before checking the solutions.

Problem 1: One-Step Inequality

Solve: x - 7 > 2

x - 7 > 2
x - 7 + 7 > 2 + 7 (Add 7 to both sides)
x > 9

Solution: All numbers greater than 9.

Problem 2: Inequality with Negative Coefficient

Solve: -3x ≥ 12

-3x ≥ 12
-3x ÷ (-3) ≤ 12 ÷ (-3) (Divide by -3, reverse the sign)
x ≤ -4

Solution: All numbers less than or equal to -4.

Problem 3: Multi-Step Inequality

Solve: 2x + 3 ≤ 4x - 5

2x + 3 ≤ 4x - 5
2x - 4x + 3 ≤ 4x - 4x - 5 (Subtract 4x from both sides)
-2x + 3 ≤ -5
-2x + 3 - 3 ≤ -5 - 3 (Subtract 3 from both sides)
-2x ≤ -8
-2x ÷ (-2) ≥ -8 ÷ (-2) (Divide by -2, reverse the sign)
x ≥ 4

Solution: All numbers greater than or equal to 4.

Problem 4: Compound Inequality

Solve: -4 < 3x + 2 ≤ 8

-4 < 3x + 2 ≤ 8
-4 - 2 < 3x + 2 - 2 ≤ 8 - 2 (Subtract 2 from all parts)
-6 < 3x ≤ 6
-6 ÷ 3 < 3x ÷ 3 ≤ 6 ÷ 3 (Divide all parts by 3)
-2 < x ≤ 2

Solution: All numbers greater than -2 and less than or equal to 2.
