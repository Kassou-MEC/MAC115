üìö What is a System of Linear Equations?

Definition: A system of linear equations is a collection of two or more linear equations that share the same variables. We're looking for values that satisfy ALL equations simultaneously!

In our case: We have two equations with two variables (usually x and y).

ü§î Think of it like dating: You need someone who meets ALL your criteria at once! No settling for "close enough" in math! üíï

üéØ Types of Solutions:

  • ‚ôæÔ∏è Infinite Solutions (Same line): The equations represent the same line
  • ‚ùå No Solution (Parallel lines): The lines never meet

üìä The Graphical Method: See It to Believe It!

üîç Graphical Method

Plot both equations on the same coordinate plane and find where they intersect!

Steps:

  1. Rewrite each equation in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b)
  2. Graph both lines
  3. Find the intersection point(s)
  4. The intersection is your solution!

üî¢ Algebraic Methods: When Graphing Gets Too Messy!

‚öîÔ∏è Two Mighty Methods:

1. Substitution Method: Solve one equation for one variable, then substitute!

2. Elimination Method: Add or subtract equations to eliminate a variable!

üîÑ Substitution Method

EASY

Example 1: Substitution

System: y = 2x + 1 and x + y = 4

Step 1: First equation gives us y = 2x + 1
Step 2: Substitute into second: x + (2x + 1) = 4
Step 3: Simplify: 3x + 1 = 4, so x = 1
Step 4: Back-substitute: y = 2(1) + 1 = 3
Solution: (1, 3) üéâ
üïµÔ∏è Substitution is like being a detective: "I'll take this clue and use it to solve the whole mystery!" üîç

‚ûï‚ûñ Elimination Method

MEDIUM

Example 2: Elimination

System: 2x + 3y = 7 and 3x - 3y = 8

Step 1: Notice y coefficients are opposites (3 and -3)
Step 2: Add equations: (2x + 3y) + (3x - 3y) = 7 + 8
Step 3: Simplify: 5x = 15, so x = 3
Step 4: Substitute back: 2(3) + 3y = 7, so y = 1/3
Solution: (3, 1/3) üéØ

üó£Ô∏è Translating English to Math

Key Strategy: Define variables, identify relationships, set up equations!

Common phrases:

EASY

Example 1: Age Problem

Problem: Sarah is 3 years older than Tom. The sum of their ages is 27. How old are they?

Variables: Let T = Tom's age, S = Sarah's age
Equations: S = T + 3 and S + T = 27
Substitute: (T + 3) + T = 27
Solve: 2T + 3 = 27, so T = 12
Answer: Tom is 12, Sarah is 15! üë∂üëß
MEDIUM

Example 2: Money Problem

Problem: A collection of 20 coins (dimes and quarters) is worth $3.05. How many of each coin?

Variables: Let d = dimes, q = quarters
Equations: d + q = 20 and 0.10d + 0.25q = 3.05
From first: d = 20 - q
Substitute: 0.10(20 - q) + 0.25q = 3.05
Solve: 2 - 0.10q + 0.25q = 3.05, so q = 7
Answer: 13 dimes, 7 quarters! üí∞
HARD

Example 3: Mixture Problem

Problem: A chemist needs 100ml of 30% acid solution. She has 20% and 50% solutions. How much of each?

Variables: Let x = ml of 20% solution, y = ml of 50% solution
Volume equation: x + y = 100
Acid equation: 0.20x + 0.50y = 0.30(100) = 30
From first: x = 100 - y
Substitute: 0.20(100 - y) + 0.50y = 30
Solve: 20 - 0.20y + 0.50y = 30, so y = 33.33ml
Answer: 66.67ml of 20%, 33.33ml of 50%! üß™
