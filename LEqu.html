Solving Linear Equations

From Basics to Advanced Problems

What are Linear Equations?

A linear equation is an algebraic equation in which each term is either a constant or the product of a constant and a single variable. Linear equations can be written in the form:

ax + b = 0

where x is the variable, and a and b are constants with a ≠ 0.

Why Learn Linear Equations?

Linear equations are fundamental in mathematics and have numerous real-world applications:

  • Calculating distances, speeds, and time
  • Determining financial calculations like interest and profit
  • Solving problems in physics and engineering
  • Analyzing data and trends in statistics

Example of a Real-World Application

If a pizza delivery service charges $3 per pizza plus a $2 delivery fee, we can represent the total cost (C) for (n) pizzas with the equation:

C = 3n + 2

If you want to know how many pizzas you can get for $20, you would solve: 3n + 2 = 20

One-Step Linear Equations

One-step equations require only one operation to solve. The goal is to isolate the variable on one side of the equation.

Basic Principles

To solve one-step equations, we use inverse operations:

  • Addition and subtraction are inverse operations
  • Multiplication and division are inverse operations

Example 1: Solving x + a = b

Solve: x + 5 = 12

x + 5 = 12
x + 5 - 5 = 12 - 5 (Subtract 5 from both sides)
x = 7

Example 2: Solving ax = b

Solve: 3x = 15

3x = 15
3x ÷ 3 = 15 ÷ 3 (Divide both sides by 3)
x = 5

Practice Problem

Solve: x - 8 = 10

x - 8 = 10
x - 8 + 8 = 10 + 8 (Add 8 to both sides)
x = 18

Two-Step Linear Equations

Two-step equations require two operations to solve. They typically involve both addition/subtraction and multiplication/division.

Solving Strategy

When solving two-step equations, follow these steps:

  1. Undo addition or subtraction
  2. Undo multiplication or division

Example 1: Solving ax + b = c

Solve: 2x + 3 = 11

2x + 3 = 11
2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3 (Subtract 3 from both sides)
2x = 8
2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2 (Divide both sides by 2)
x = 4

Example 2: Solving x/a - b = c

Solve: x/4 - 2 = 3

x/4 - 2 = 3
x/4 - 2 + 2 = 3 + 2 (Add 2 to both sides)
x/4 = 5
x/4 × 4 = 5 × 4 (Multiply both sides by 4)
x = 20

Practice Problem

Solve: 5x - 7 = 18

5x - 7 = 18
5x - 7 + 7 = 18 + 7 (Add 7 to both sides)
5x = 25
5x ÷ 5 = 25 ÷ 5 (Divide both sides by 5)
x = 5

Multi-Step Linear Equations

Multi-step equations require multiple operations to solve. They may involve combining like terms, using the distributive property, or variables on both sides.

Solving Strategy

When solving multi-step equations, follow these steps:

  1. Simplify both sides of the equation (distribute, combine like terms)
  2. Move variable terms to one side
  3. Undo addition or subtraction
  4. Undo multiplication or division

Example 1: Using the Distributive Property

Solve: 3(x + 2) = 21

3(x + 2) = 21
3x + 6 = 21 (Distribute 3)
3x + 6 - 6 = 21 - 6 (Subtract 6 from both sides)
3x = 15
3x ÷ 3 = 15 ÷ 3 (Divide both sides by 3)
x = 5

Example 2: Variables on Both Sides

Solve: 4x + 3 = 2x + 11

4x + 3 = 2x + 11
4x - 2x + 3 = 2x - 2x + 11 (Subtract 2x from both sides)
2x + 3 = 11
2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3 (Subtract 3 from both sides)
2x = 8
2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2 (Divide both sides by 2)
x = 4

Practice Problem

Solve: 2(3x - 5) = 4x + 6

2(3x - 5) = 4x + 6
6x - 10 = 4x + 6 (Distribute 2)
6x - 4x - 10 = 4x - 4x + 6 (Subtract 4x from both sides)
2x - 10 = 6
2x - 10 + 10 = 6 + 10 (Add 10 to both sides)
2x = 16
2x ÷ 2 = 16 ÷ 2 (Divide both sides by 2)
x = 8

Linear Equations with Fractions

Equations with fractions can be solved by eliminating the fractions first. Multiply both sides of the equation by the least common denominator (LCD) of all fractions.

Solving Strategy

When solving equations with fractions:

  1. Find the LCD of all fractions
  2. Multiply every term on both sides by the LCD
  3. Solve the resulting equation

Example 1: Simple Fraction Equations

Solve: (1/2)x + 3 = 7

(1/2)x + 3 = 7
2 × [(1/2)x + 3] = 2 × 7 (Multiply both sides by 2)
x + 6 = 14
x + 6 - 6 = 14 - 6 (Subtract 6 from both sides)
x = 8

Example 2: Multiple Fractions

Solve: (x/3) - (1/4) = (5/6)

(x/3) - (1/4) = (5/6)
LCD of 3, 4, and 6 is 12
12 × (x/3) - 12 × (1/4) = 12 × (5/6) (Multiply all terms by 12)
4x - 3 = 10
4x - 3 + 3 = 10 + 3 (Add 3 to both sides)
4x = 13
4x ÷ 4 = 13 ÷ 4 (Divide both sides by 4)
x = 13/4 or 3.25

Practice Problem

Solve: (2x/5) + (1/3) = 4

(2x/5) + (1/3) = 4
LCD of 5 and 3 is 15
15 × (2x/5) + 15 × (1/3) = 15 × 4 (Multiply all terms by 15)
6x + 5 = 60
6x + 5 - 5 = 60 - 5 (Subtract 5 from both sides)
6x = 55
6x ÷ 6 = 55 ÷ 6 (Divide both sides by 6)
x = 55/6 or approximately 9.167

Practice Problems

Problem 1: One-Step Equation

Solve: x + 9 = 15

x + 9 = 15
x + 9 - 9 = 15 - 9
x = 6

Problem 2: Two-Step Equation

Solve: 3y - 7 = 14

3y - 7 = 14
3y - 7 + 7 = 14 + 7
3y = 21
3y ÷ 3 = 21 ÷ 3
y = 7

Problem 3: Multi-Step Equation

Solve: 2(x + 3) = 5x - 3

2(x + 3) = 5x - 3
2x + 6 = 5x - 3
2x - 5x + 6 = 5x - 5x - 3
-3x + 6 = -3
-3x + 6 - 6 = -3 - 6
-3x = -9
-3x ÷ -3 = -9 ÷ -3
x = 3

Problem 4: Equation with Fractions

Solve: (3x/4) - (1/2) = (5/8)

(3x/4) - (1/2) = (5/8)
LCD of 4, 2, and 8 is 8
8 × (3x/4) - 8 × (1/2) = 8 × (5/8)
6x - 4 = 5
6x - 4 + 4 = 5 + 4
6x = 9
6x ÷ 6 = 9 ÷ 6
x = 9/6 = 3/2 or 1.5
